The operating system for Ghanaian laundries. This page is the visual foundation — the wordmark, the living o, the palette, the type, and the components every screen is built from.

01 — The mark

One shape. Every job.

The wordmark carries no separate icon. Instead the o is built heavier than the letters around it, with a single notch cut at the top-right — the rinse notch. Pulled out of the word, that notched ring is unmistakably Rinsion. It prints in pure black, it lifts off to spin, it collapses to a status dot, and it opens up to frame a pickup code.

Handoff notes · For the build phase

Deliberate deviations from the brief.

Where the build differs from the written brief on purpose. Keep these as designed — they are not oversights to "correct" back to spec.

Reports — keep the bar charts

The brief specifies "stats and tables in v1; no charts" for the Reports screen. The build intentionally adds three lightweight CSS bar charts — revenue this week, revenue by branch, and orders by status. They are pure HTML/CSS (no charting library, no new dependency) and were reviewed and approved as a deliberate deviation because they make the data scannable at a glance.

Build action: ship the charts as designed. Do not strip Reports back to plain tables.

Approved 29 Jun 2026 · Mobile back buttons also bumped to 44×44 to meet the brief's primary-navigation hit-target minimum.
