01 — The mark

One shape. Every job.

The wordmark carries no separate icon. The o is built heavier than the letters around it, with a single notch cut at the top-right — the rinse notch. Pulled out of the word, that notched ring is unmistakably Rinsion. It prints in pure black, it spins to load, it collapses to a status dot, and it opens up to frame a pickup code.

10 — Codes

Two codes, two jobs.

The long order id is for the shop and the audit trail. The short pickup code is the only thing the customer ever sees or reads aloud. Never mix them.

Order id — internal
ORD-4KP7MX2A
  • ORD- prefix + 8 uppercase chars.
  • Shown in tables, detail headers, the log & exports.
  • Unique forever; never reused.
Pickup code — the customer's
K7MQ2P
  • 6 chars, no confusable 0/O/1/I.
  • Goes in the SMS & on the counter screen.
  • Short enough to read over a phone call.
11 — Feedback & destructive actions

The more it costs to undo, the louder we get.

Toasts confirm the reversible. Banners carry account-level issues. Modals stop you before anything you can't take back — deleting or cancelling an order names the customer and states the consequence plainly.

Toasts — brief, with an undo where it makes sense
Order marked Ready. Text sent to Ama.
Order deleted.
Banners — account & billing
Your subscription expired 3 days ago. You have 3 days of read-only access left.
You've used 340 / 400 texts this month. Overage bills at GHS 0.05 each.
Destructive confirmations
Cancel this order?
Akosua Boateng's order ORD-M6YJT9ZK will be marked cancelled. The customer won't be notified. This can't be undone.
Rules for destructive actions
  • Always a modal, never an inline one-tap.
  • Name the record and the person it belongs to.
  • State the consequence — including whether the customer is told.
  • Confirm button is red and repeats the verb (“Cancel order”), never just “OK”.
  • Where safe, prefer soft-delete + an Undo toast over a hard confirm.

Handoff notes · For the build phase

How to use this file.

This is the only source of truth

This file replaces the old Brand Foundations entirely. If a screen doesn't match what's here — radius, codes, status colour, spacing — the screen is wrong, not this.

Reports keep the bar charts

The three lightweight CSS bar charts on Reports are an approved deviation. Ship them; don't strip Reports back to plain tables.

Responsive is not optional

Every pattern here has a phone form. Tables become cards; sidebar becomes a bottom bar; primary action becomes a FAB. Build both.

Rinsion — Design System · Light mode. Dark mode is future work: invert to #14140F surfaces, keep emerald & clay hues, re-check contrast on status pills.
